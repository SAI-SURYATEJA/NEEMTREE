ABOUT TOEFL

The TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) is a widely recognized English language proficiency test, accepted by over 11,000 educational institutions, professional associations, immigration authorities, scholarship providers, and organizations in 150 countries. It is the most globally accepted language assessment and is offered at 4,500 test centers across 165+ countries. Administered by the non-profit ETS (Educational Testing Service), the TOEFL iBT (Internet Based Test) evaluates the English language skills of non-native speakers, assessing their ability to comprehend and use English effectively in academic settings. Students can take the TOEFL multiple times, with a minimum 12-day gap between attempts.

FAQ's

Student Questions

What is the TOEFL exam, and why do I need to take it for studying abroad?

The TOEFL exam is a test of English language proficiency required by many universities as proof of your ability to study in an English-speaking academic environment. It assesses your reading, listening, speaking, and writing skills.

How do I register for the TOEFL exam?

You can register for the TOEFL exam online through the ETS website. We can also assist you in the registration process.

What is the format of the TOEFL IBT (Internet Based Test)?

The TOEFL iBT consists of four sections: Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing. It is administered via the internet, and you'll complete these sections on a computer.

How long is the TOEFL exam, and how is it scored?

The TOEFL iBT typically lasts about 3.5 to 4 hours. Scores range from 0 to 120, with separate scores for each section.

When and where can I take the TOEFL exam?

The TOEFL is offered year-round, and you can choose a test date and location that suits you. There are numerous test centers globally, and we can help you find a convenient one.

Is there a minimum TOEFL score required for admission to universities?

Different universities have varying score requirements. It's essential to check the specific requirements for each university you plan to apply to.

How can I prepare for the TOEFL exam?

You can prepare for the TOEFL through self-study using official ETS materials or by enrolling in a TOEFL preparation course, which we can recommend.

Can I retake the TOEFL if I'm not satisfied with my score?

Yes, you can retake the TOEFL, but there is a 12-day waiting period between attempts. We can help you plan your retake if necessary.

Are there accommodations available for test-takers with disabilities or special needs?

Yes, ETS provides accommodations for test-takers with documented disabilities. You'll need to request these accommodations in advance.

How long are TOEFL scores valid?

TOEFL scores are typically valid for two years, but some institutions may have different score expiration policies. It's essential to check with your chosen universities.
