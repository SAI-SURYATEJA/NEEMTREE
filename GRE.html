About GRE

The Graduate Record Exam (GRE) is the go-to admission test for graduate and business schools in the United States and other prominent study destinations worldwide. Educational Testing Service (ETS), a non-profit organization, oversees the administration of the GRE. This comprehensive exam evaluates a test-taker's critical thinking, quantitative reasoning, verbal reasoning, and analytical writing skills. Universities use GRE scores in conjunction with academic achievements to assess candidates' readiness for advanced studies. Aspiring graduate and business school students can sit for the GRE once every 21 days, with a maximum of five attempts within any consecutive 12-month period. This flexibility allows applicants ample opportunities to showcase their academic prowess.

FAQ's

Student Questions

What is the GRE, and why is it important for my study abroad journey?

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is a standardized test that assesses your readiness for graduate-level studies. Many universities require GRE scores as part of the admission process, making it crucial for your application.

When should I start preparing for the GRE?

It's advisable to begin your GRE preparation at least 2-3 months before your desired test date to allow for comprehensive review and practice.

How do I register for the GRE, and what is the cost?

You can register for the GRE online through the ETS website. The cost varies by location, but as of my last update, it was approximately $205.

How can your consultancy assist with GRE preparation?

We offer personalized GRE coaching, study plans, practice materials, and expert guidance to ensure you perform at your best on the test.

What's a competitive GRE score for my chosen universities?

The required score varies by institution and program. We'll help you identify the score range you should aim for based on your university choices.

Can you provide guidance on how to send my GRE scores to universities?

Yes, we can assist you in sending your GRE scores to the universities of your choice. We'll explain the process and help you with the necessary steps.

How many times can I take the GRE, and how do retakes affect my application?

You can take the GRE up to five times within any continuous 12-month period, with a 21-day gap between attempts. Most universities consider your highest score, so retaking the test can improve your application.

Do I need to take the GRE for all graduate programs?

Not necessarily. Some programs and universities may waive the GRE requirement, particularly for certain master's programs. We can help you identify which programs do not require the GRE.

How do I obtain a GRE fee waiver?

Fee waivers are available for eligible individuals. We can guide you on the eligibility criteria and the application process for obtaining a GRE fee waiver.

Can you help with visa and application processes for studying abroad?

Yes, we offer comprehensive assistance with visa applications and the entire university application process, ensuring a smooth transition to your chosen institution.
